Eponymous Streets of NYC

A Typographic Word Map that accentuates The Lengths of "Eponymous Streets of NYC" on a circular diagram.
The size of type relates to the length of street/avenue in New York City. The map is organized clockwise
starting from 1600 to the 2000.

  • Created for Core Studio Typography with William M. Bevington at Parsons School of Design.

NYC Secrets

New York City Secrets is developed based on the dice game framework; the system responds to user’s chances out of twelve New York City’s hidden places. The next page changes over the course of the user’s visit.

Parrhesia

Parrhesia is a web application that uses scientists Anthony Greenwald (University of Washington),
Mahzarin Banaji (Harvard University), and Brian Nosek (University of Virginia) questions to provide
its users an anonymous and public insight into their implicit biases. It does not try to find out if you are
biased, but rather to measure the strength of a person’s automatic associations under the assumption
that everyone is biased, even if they have never done anything biased.

About

Baki Mumkaya is a New York-based multi-disciplinary designer. He was born in Adana, Turkey and graduated from Kyrenia American University in Cyprus with a degree in MIS in 2006. During his study, he joined GAU as a Creative Design Director to lead the design projects for University's expanding colleges. After moving to New York, he pursued his dreams in creative design at Parsons School for Design. Baki also established his own practice in both New York and Istanbul. His clients include A.R.E. Set Design, XDEA, Chic Of a Kind and RDG Foundation. He recently worked with a fast growing research and development start-up Human Condition Labs, assisting clients to achieve their financial goals by building their brands and products. Some clients: Moral Courage, Skin OS, HC Safety, Goren Group and Lady Passé. He also serves on the board of National Kidney Services; a non-profit organization devoted to raising funds for kidney disease research, education, early detection, organ donation, and patient advocacy with offices in Charlotte, North Carolina and Columbus, Ohio.
